## 1. Geoff — Founder, Perfectia Productions

> At first it was annoying because my nervous system is wired for
> action, movement and immediate gratification. The more I worked it
> through, the more I got the fruits of the interaction. It's true to
> its name. It's forcing you to turn inward. It forces the slowdown
> and reflection and intentionally gets you uncomfortable — but not
> unseen. This is where many coaches and therapists get it wrong. The
> tough love approach that doesn't actually meet you where you are.
> This one says, 'I see you exactly for who you are. Here's why you
> think it. Here's why it's holding you back.' Then gives you the
> space to dig yourself out.

## 2. Amber Stubbs, PhD

> It's slower at times than Claude, ChatGPT, or Grok: but hands down

## 3. Anonymous beta tester

> I've been married for over 15 years and I've made peace with the
> fact that my husband can be mean at times. I'm not leaving and I'm
> not trying to fix him. What I'm trying to do is stay clear about
> what's mine and what's his: which with my trauma history is harder
> than it sounds. Most coaching either pushed me toward a decision
> (leave) or flooded me with reframes that don't actually meet what
> I'm working on. InnerVerse does something different. It meets me
> where I actually am. When the system says something like 'your line
> is contact where there is respect, and space where there is not,'
> that's not a platitude: that's exactly the work.
>
> It supports the work I'm actually doing instead of trying to
> redirect me to the work it thinks I should be doing.
